A Little About Me

Important: This is my personal home page. If you're looking for information about my research or academic pursuits, please see my academic home page at http://academic.patrick.wagstrom.net/ and bookmark that page instead. It's much better organized for folks interested in my work. This page contains lots of information about my life when I'm not working.
This is the requisite bit of information about me. I'm a 29 year old graduate student in the Department of Engineering and Public Policy at Carnegie Mellon University. I'm currently looking at various aspects of software policy, specifically related to open source software and community creation. But that's subject to change. In addition to what I look at for research, I'm also interested in various issues related to liberty and technology and follow debates on the PATRIOT act, HDTV broadcast flag, and politics in general.

I moved to Pittsburgh in August 2003 after spending six years in Chicago. There I attended Illinois Institute of Technology where I attained bachelors of science degrees in Computer Science, Computer Engineering, Electrical Engineering and a masters of science degree in Computer Science. While there I did a number of interesting things, such as working for Argonne National Laboratory, managing the computer systems for an advertising agency, worked for a startup, and other assorted mayhem.

Among other interesting facts is that I was recently married to Kristina Felfe. You can find lots of pictures from it up on our photos page. A bit of a recap can be found on wedding.wagstrom.net.
