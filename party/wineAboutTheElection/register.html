Wine About the Election

Once every four years Americans have a civic duty to travel to their local polling place, pick between the several candidates, each of which is perfectly capable letting down everyone who voted for them by not implementing anything they said they would. What's the only solution? Wine about the election. We invite all those interested to please come and "Wine" about the election on Tuesday, November 4th from 7:30pm - 1am (sorry folks, I'm not staying up all night like I did in 2000 and 2004). Here's all the information you need to know:

Your polling place is at: 3938 Nantasket St, Pittsburgh, PA 15207 (map)
The polls are open from: 7:30pm on November 4th, 2008 - 1:00am on November 5th, 2008
Voter Hotline: If you have questions please contact one of the election judges. Either Patrick (412-606-9730, patrick@wagstrom.net) or Kristina (412-606-1633, kristina@wagstrom.net)
To vote you must: Register to vote (see form at the bottom of this page)
Requirements for entering the polling place: Proper classy attire for wine-ing about the election is required of all those entering the polling place. All guests are encouraged to bring items consistent with the party they registered with. So, if you're bringing soda, register as a prohibitionist, white wine should be "chilled whites", etc.
Deadline for registration: We allow day of and on-site registration. We prefer that, if possible, guests register no later than November 2nd, 2008.
Access to voter rolls: All interested individuals may examine the voter rolls to verify that they are registered and to see who else is registered. In the interest of creating a balanced event and avoiding a two-party system, new individuals may wish to examine the rolls before registering.
Avoiding politics: We'll have the TV on covering results upstairs, while downstairs will be a no-spin zone if you just want to avoid that stuff.

As a final note, all individuals of all political persuasions may wine about the election -- unless you voted for Nader in Florida in 2000. While wine-ing about the election is a non-partisan event, the election judges do have their preference in candidates, and may get mopey or ecstatice depending on if said candidate wins or loses. We ask that individuals please refrain from wearing articles of clothing for political candidates, unless it's something kitchy, like an "I like Ike" button -- of course, your pitted out Wellstone '90 t-shirt is not appropriate attire for wine-ing about the election.

Election Pick'em: To give people a little more to do, I've put up a form where you can try your hand at picking which candidate will win each state. Go fill out the form and pick between undecided, Obama, and McCain. The winner will be determined by the election results at Midnight. All responses must be received by 5:00pm on Tuesday, November 4th to be entered.
Clarification: So, trying to be all coy with the theme has caused a few problems, so here's the non-coded instructions. Fill out the form below to RSVP for the party, include other folks on the "Other Individuals" line. The party registration is used to indicate what you're going to bring to the party. Also, this is a wine tasting/party, so we ask that people dress appropriately for such an event. Don't worry, it confused Kristina too.
